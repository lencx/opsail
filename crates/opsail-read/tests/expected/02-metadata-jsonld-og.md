# River Observatory Reopens After Spring Repairs

The hillside river observatory reopened this week after crews replaced damaged walkways, calibrated water-level sensors, and rebuilt the small shelter used by visiting researchers. The station will publish a fresh reading every morning and will keep monthly summaries in a public archive.

Staff members said the repair period also gave them time to document the collection process. Each reading now includes the observation time, recent rainfall, instrument status, and a short note explaining interruptions. Those details should make seasonal comparisons easier for local readers.

Weekend visits remain limited to small groups because the final section of the access path is narrow. Reservations are optional on ordinary weekdays, and the outdoor gauge display can be viewed even when the indoor room is closed. A later update will describe the autumn workshop schedule.
